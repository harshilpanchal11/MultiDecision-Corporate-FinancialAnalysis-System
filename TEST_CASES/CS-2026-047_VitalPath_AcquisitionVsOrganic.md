## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

VitalPath Health, Inc. is a Series C-stage digital health company headquartered in Nashville, Tennessee, offering an AI-powered patient engagement and care coordination platform to mid-market and enterprise health systems across the United States. Founded in March 2019 by CEO Dr. Priya Mehta and CTO Marcus Webb, the company has grown to $42.4 million in annualized recurring revenue (ARR) as of Q1 2026—representing 67% year-over-year growth—and serves 218 health system clients ranging from regional community hospitals to integrated delivery networks (IDNs). VitalPath's platform integrates with major electronic health record (EHR) systems (Epic, Cerner, Meditech) and automates multi-channel patient outreach using proprietary NLP models trained on clinical notes.

The company's Board of Directors faces a time-sensitive capital allocation decision: whether to accelerate entry into the rapidly expanding chronic disease management (CDM) software market through the acquisition of ChronoCare Solutions, Inc.—a Nashville-based CDM-focused SaaS company with $11.8 million ARR and 48% year-over-year growth (Option A)—or to invest $22.4 million in an 18-month organic product development initiative internally codenamed "Project Chronos" (Option B). The CDM market is expanding at a 23% CAGR and represents VitalPath's most natural product adjacency, given its existing relationships with 218 health systems. Critically, 22 of those accounts have explicitly flagged CDM capability gaps during renewal discussions, presenting both an immediate churn risk and a cross-sell opportunity that will erode if left unaddressed.

Key constraints shaping the decision include: (1) a current cash position of $34.2 million yielding an 18.5-month runway at current burn rates of $1.85 million per month; (2) $8.0 million in outstanding venture debt carrying a financial covenant requiring a minimum cash balance of $12.0 million at all times; (3) a Board-imposed maximum dilution threshold of 18% per financing event; and (4) the need to resolve this strategic question before VitalPath's anticipated Series D fundraising process commences in Q3 2026. Option A is structured as $40.0 million cash plus $22.0 million in VitalPath stock (total EV: $62.0 million at 5.25x ARR), and requires a concurrent minimum $28.0 million Series D raise to maintain covenant compliance. Option B preserves near-term cash and cap-table flexibility but delivers materially lower ARR by Month 24 ($96.3 million vs. $115.7 million) and likely misses the Board's stated $100 million ARR target for Q4 2027.

The Board must render a decision by May 15, 2026—five days before VitalPath's 30-day exclusivity agreement with ChronoCare expires. Delay beyond this window allows ChronoCare to re-engage other potential acquirers, potentially closing the acquisition path entirely.

## COMPANY BACKGROUND

### History and Current Position

VitalPath Health, Inc. was founded in March 2019 by Dr. Priya Mehta, former VP of Clinical Informatics at HCA Healthcare, and Marcus Webb, former engineering lead at Teladoc Health. The company's initial product was a care gap identification and outreach automation tool sold to regional hospital systems, addressing a critical operational pain point: health systems were losing an estimated $180–$240 billion annually in value-based contract revenue attributable to poor chronic condition monitoring and inadequate patient re-engagement workflows. VitalPath's platform automated multi-channel outreach—SMS, in-app messaging, and care coordinator task queues—using proprietary NLP models trained on de-identified clinical notes, reducing care gap closure timelines by an average of 43% across its early install base.

The company raised a $6.2 million Series A in March 2020 led by NEA, and a $22 million Series B in January 2022 led by General Atlantic, using the capital to expand its sales team from 8 to 34 account executives and deepen its Epic App Orchard integration. A $52 million Series C closed in October 2024 led by Andreessen Horowitz Health, which funded headcount expansion to 187 employees and geographic penetration from 12 to 29 states. As of Q1 2026, VitalPath's platform is actively used by 218 health systems representing approximately 38 million attributed patients. Enterprise contracts average 2.4 years in initial term with structured annual escalators of 4–6%, and the company's 119% net revenue retention reflects strong land-and-expand dynamics.

Despite strong top-line momentum, VitalPath operates in an increasingly competitive landscape. Larger platform players—including Health Catalyst, Innovaccer, and Arcadia—have begun embedding patient engagement modules directly into their population health suites, applying pricing pressure on VitalPath's existing accounts. More urgently, VitalPath's current product architecture does not include dedicated CDM workflows: structured disease registries, clinical quality measure (CQM) tracking, care plan management, or HEDIS gap closure modules. Approximately 68% of VitalPath's existing customers have indicated interest in CDM capabilities in renewal discussions, and 22 customers have explicitly stated they are actively evaluating standalone CDM vendors—representing $4.3 million in at-risk ARR and a cross-sell TAM of $33.0 million within the existing install base alone.

## THE DECISION

VitalPath's executive team and Board have narrowed the company's strategic options for capturing the chronic disease management opportunity to two paths. Both options are framed around a 24-month primary horizon (through Q1 2028), with Month 36 projections provided for sensitivity testing. Each option requires a concurrent equity raise; the question is the size, structure, and strategic context of that capital event.

### Option A: Acquisition of ChronoCare Solutions, Inc.

ChronoCare sells exclusively to health systems and ACOs on annual SaaS contracts, with an average contract value of $166K per year. The company has 72 employees, 71 active health system clients, and crucially, 12 of those clients are also active VitalPath customers—representing an immediate cross-sell surface area at contract renewal.

### Option B: Organic Build — Project Chronos

#### Program Overview

Project Chronos is a structured 18-month internal product development initiative to build a native CDM module within VitalPath's existing platform. The program would add disease registry management, HEDIS gap-closure workflows, structured care plan templates, and CQM dashboards to VitalPath's current outreach and engagement suite—leveraging existing EHR integrations and the company's ML data pipeline. The CDM module would be sold to existing customers as an add-on at an incremental ACV of $80–$120K per customer (positioned below ChronoCare's $166K standalone ACV as a feature-tier offering, with full-suite CDM breadth achieved by Month 24).

## KEY DECISION FACTORS

### Strategic Objectives (from Board)

1. **Market Share — CDM Penetration:** Capture CDM capabilities in a minimum of 8% of VitalPath's existing 218-customer install base (minimum 18 customers; stretch target: 35 customers) with an active CDM product by Q4 2027.

3. **NRR Defense:** Maintain company-wide Net Revenue Retention above 115% through Q4 2027; CDM expansion must not cannibalize core platform NRR through integration complexity or customer confusion.

## QUESTIONS FOR ANALYSIS

### Primary Question:

Should VitalPath Health, Inc. acquire ChronoCare Solutions for $62.0 million (Option A) or execute the $22.4 million organic Project Chronos build (Option B) to capture the chronic disease management market, and under what specific financial and operational conditions would the preferred option underperform the alternative?

### Secondary Questions:

1. **Valuation and Return Justification:** At a 5.25x ARR entry multiple, is VitalPath overpaying for ChronoCare? Using a discounted incremental ARR methodology with a 12% cost of capital, what is the net present value of the $19.4 million ARR premium from Option A at Month 24? At what acquisition multiple does Option A generate equivalent NPV to Option B, and does the current deal price fall below that breakeven multiple?

2. **Cross-Sell Synergy Achievability:** Option A's base case projects 62 cross-sell customers at $116K incremental ACV by Month 24 ($7.2M ARR in synergies). If only 30–35 cross-sell customers are achieved (the downside case), does Option A still generate a higher risk-adjusted NPV than Option B? What is the minimum cross-sell conversion rate required—expressed as a percentage of VitalPath's existing 218-customer base—to justify the $62.0M acquisition price?

3. **Integration Risk Quantification:** Marcus Webb (CTO) estimates 18–24 months for full technical product integration versus the CEO's 12-month base case assumption.
