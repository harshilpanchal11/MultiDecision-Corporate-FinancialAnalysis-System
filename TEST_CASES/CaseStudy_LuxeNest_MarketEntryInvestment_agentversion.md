## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

In March 2026, Sophie Tran, CEO of LuxeNest Home, must decide whether her company will enter the Southeast Asian market. LuxeNest is a US-based premium home furnishings and décor e-commerce brand, generating $210 million in annual revenue with a loyal customer base of design-conscious millennials and Gen Z homeowners. Having saturated its US growth runway, the board has identified Southeast Asia — specifically Singapore as a gateway market, followed by Indonesia and Vietnam — as the highest-potential international expansion opportunity.

After 8 months of market research, the strategy and finance teams have developed a single entry plan: a premium brand launch into Singapore in Year 1, expanding to affluent urban segments in Indonesia and Vietnam in Years 2 and 3. The plan requires a $28 million investment over 36 months and carries a projected NPV of $31.4 million at LuxeNest's WACC of 10.2%.

The question before the board is not how to enter — that has been decided. The question is whether to enter at all.

The board meets in 45 days. Sophie must walk in with a GO or NO-GO recommendation backed by a full investment case.

## COMPANY BACKGROUND

### History and Current Position

LuxeNest Home was founded in 2015 in Austin, Texas, by Sophie Tran (CEO, former RISD design graduate and Wayfair product lead) and co-founder Marcus Holden (COO, former Amazon logistics). ### Why Southeast Asia — and Why Now

The board's interest in Southeast Asia is driven by five converging factors:

1. **Middle class expansion**: Southeast Asia's middle class is projected to grow from 190 million to 350 million by 2035, with the fastest growth in Indonesia (population 278 million), Vietnam, and the Philippines
2. **E-commerce infrastructure maturity**: Shopee, Lazada, and Tokopedia have built last-mile delivery infrastructure across Tier-1 and Tier-2 cities — reducing the barrier to digital-first market entry
3. **Home category growth**: Post-pandemic home improvement spending has accelerated across SEA; the home furnishings e-commerce category grew 31% in 2025
4. **US market saturation signals**: LuxeNest's US growth has decelerated from 38% (2022) to 14% (2025) as the brand approaches penetration limits in its target demographic
5. **Competitive window**: No dominant premium Western home brand has established a strong foothold in SEA — the window may close within 24–36 months as IKEA accelerates its regional expansion and regional players professionalise

## THE ENTRY PLAN

### Strategy Overview

LuxeNest will enter Southeast Asia as a fully positioned premium brand, mirroring the model that built its US business. Singapore is the entry point: small, affluent ($65,000 GDP per capita), English-speaking, and home to the regional headquarters of most multinational brands. A successful Singapore launch validates the brand for the broader region and provides a logistics and fulfilment hub for subsequent market entries into Indonesia and Vietnam.

The plan requires a flagship experiential showroom on Orchard Road (Singapore's premier retail corridor), a localised e-commerce platform, regional marketing investment, and a curated inventory of 420 SKUs adapted for tropical climates and smaller living spaces (average Singapore apartment: 1,000 sq. ft. vs. US average 2,300 sq. ft.).

**Entry Markets and Sequencing:**
- Year 1: Singapore (gateway market — full brand launch, flagship store + DTC)
- Year 2: Indonesia affluent urban (Jakarta, Surabaya — DTC only, no physical retail)
- Year 3: Vietnam affluent urban (Ho Chi Minh City — DTC only)

**Brand:** LuxeNest Home — full brand, no sub-brand, premium positioning maintained across all markets.

## ADDITIONAL CONTEXT

### Board Composition and Perspectives

1. **Sophie Tran (CEO, Co-founder)** — Leans GO
   - "This is the right market at the right moment. Singapore gives us a controlled entry, and the premium segment is underserved. We built LuxeNest on the belief that design commands a premium anywhere."
   - Concern: "We have never operated internationally. If we execute poorly in Singapore, we close the regional window ourselves."

2. **Marcus Holden (COO, Co-founder)** — Cautiously GO
   - "The logistics infrastructure in SEA has matured enough to make this viable. My concern is execution speed — we need the right regional leadership before we commit."
   - Concern: "28 million dollars is 64% of our cash. If US growth softens simultaneously, we are stretched."

3. **Diane Park (CMO)** — Strongly GO
   - "Castlery just raised $50M and is moving into Indonesia. Every month we wait, the premium shelf space narrows. The brand will travel — Singapore proves it."
   - Concern: "The 5.2-year payback is long. We need to make sure the board has the patience to see it through."

4. **Daniel Kwan (CFO)** — Leans NO-GO
   - "We have $44M in cash. This plan spends $28M over 36 months — but the cumulative cash outflow before breakeven is $46.9M. We may need to draw on our credit facility in Year 2. That changes our risk profile materially."
   - Concern: "The IRR of 22.7% is above our hurdle, but the discounted payback of 6.9 years is beyond our 5-year planning horizon. If anything goes wrong in Year 3, we have limited ability to course-correct."

5. **Ingrid Sorensen (Lead Investor, Sequoia)** — GO
   - "LuxeNest's valuation is built on premium brand equity and a compelling growth narrative. US growth at 14% is slowing. The SEA story is exactly what the next funding round or exit needs."
   - Concern: "Make sure the Singapore flagship is operationally excellent from Day 1. A stumble there is visible in a way that a digital stumble is not."

6. **Ray Mendoza (CSO)** — GO, with conditions
   - "The market data is compelling. I'd feel better with a formal review gate at Month 18 — if Singapore isn't tracking to plan by then, we pause the Indonesia entry and preserve capital."
   - Concern: "Brand awareness of 6% in Singapore today means we're starting from zero. The marketing budget needs to be protected, not the first thing cut if we're behind plan."

## KEY DECISION FACTORS

### Strategic Objectives (from Board)
1. **Restore revenue growth trajectory** — US growth decelerating to 14%; SEA adds a second growth engine targeting $85M revenue by Year 5
2. **Preserve brand equity** — Any entry must be consistent with LuxeNest's premium positioning; brand dilution is not acceptable
3. **Maintain financial sustainability** — Investment must not impair US operations; minimum $10M cash reserve must be maintained
4. **Build a durable competitive moat** — Entry must establish first-mover advantage in premium SEA home DTC before Castlery or a US incumbent closes the window
5. **Deliver positive NPV above the cost of capital** — IRR must exceed 15% threshold for international expansion investment class

### Financial Metrics Summary

| Metric | Value | Assessment |
|---|---|---|
| NPV (@ 10.2% WACC, 7yr) | $31.4M | Positive — value-creating above cost of capital |
| IRR | 22.7% | Above typical 15–20% international expansion hurdle |
| Payback Period | 5.2 years | Long — exceeds 5-year board planning horizon by 2–3 months |
| Discounted Payback | 6.9 years | Extended — reflects heavy front-loaded investment |
| Peak Cash Outflow | $46.9M (cumulative through Year 3) | Requires partial credit facility use in Year 2 |
| Cash Remaining (post Year 1) | ~$19M + credit facility | Adequate but thin if US EBITDA softens |
| Year 5 SEA Revenue | $85.4M | ~41% of current US revenue — material second business |

### Qualitative Factors
- **Organisational readiness**: LuxeNest has never operated internationally — no regional leadership team, no import/logistics infrastructure, no SEA legal entity yet established. These are 6–9 month lead time items before any revenue can be generated
- **Brand portability**: Consumer research shows 41% of Singapore's target income segment is willing to pay a premium for a US design brand — comparable to the 38% who were receptive to Castlery's Singapore-origin brand at launch
- **Competitive window**: Castlery's January 2026 funding round signals the premium SEA home segment is investable and contested — the window for first-mover advantage in Singapore is likely 18–24 months
- **Execution dependency**: The entire regional rollout — Indonesia in Year 2, Vietnam in Year 3 — is contingent on Singapore performing at or above plan by Month 18. Singapore is not just a gateway; it is a load-bearing assumption for the entire financial model
