## Meridian Analytics: The Refinancing Dilemma

### Case Study Number: CS-2026-005
### Industry: Technology / SaaS (B2B Analytics)
### Core Tension: Refinancing Existing Debt vs. Raising Equity at a Discount
### Date: March 2026
### Prepared by: Corporate Finance Analysis Project

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

In March 2026, Priya Anand, CEO of Meridian Analytics, has 60 days to close a financing transaction. Meridian, a B2B SaaS company providing supply chain intelligence software to mid-market manufacturers, is carrying $48 million in high-cost venture debt taken on during the 2022 growth boom — debt that matures in September 2026. With $9 million in cash and a monthly burn of $1.1 million, the runway math is unambiguous: Meridian cannot retire the debt from operations alone.

Two financing paths are on the table:

**Option A: Debt Refinancing** — Replace the maturing $48M venture debt with a new $52M term loan from a private credit fund at 13.5% interest, secured against ARR and IP. Preserves full founder and investor equity but loads the balance sheet with expensive, covenant-heavy debt during a period of uncertain growth.

**Option B: Discounted Equity Round** — Raise $38M in a Series C equity round led by a crossover investor at a $95M pre-money valuation — a 41% discount to Meridian's last round valuation of $162M in 2022. Retires the debt, funds 24 months of operations, but severely dilutes existing shareholders and resets the cap table at a painful markdown.

Neither option is comfortable. Option A preserves valuation optics but risks financial distress if growth falters. Option B solves the balance sheet but crystallizes a humiliating down round that may trigger employee option underwater issues, investor markdowns, and customer confidence questions.

The board meets in 60 days. Priya must walk in with a recommendation.

## COMPANY BACKGROUND

### History and Current Position

Meridian Analytics was founded in 2019 by Priya Anand (CEO, former McKinsey supply chain practice) and co-founder James Okafor (CTO, former Amazon logistics engineer). The company's core product — MeridianIQ — is a SaaS platform that ingests ERP, logistics, and supplier data to provide real-time supply chain visibility, demand forecasting, and disruption alerting for mid-market manufacturers.

Meridian raised a $162M Series B in April 2022 at a $162M post-money valuation (~12x the company's ARR at the time, reflecting peak 2022 SaaS multiples), riding the height of the SaaS valuation bubble. The round included $40M in venture debt alongside $122M in equity, on the assumption that rapid ARR growth would make the debt service trivial within 18 months.

That assumption did not hold. Growth slowed from 85% to 28% YoY as customers tightened budgets post-2022. Churn rose. The debt remains, and the clock is ticking.

## THE TWO FINANCING OPTIONS

---

### Option A: Debt Refinancing — New $52M Term Loan

**Counterparty:** Blue Arch Credit Partners (private credit fund, $4.2B AUM)  
**Structure:** Senior secured term loan  
**Amount:** $52 million (retires $48M existing debt + $4M cash to balance sheet)

#### Term Sheet Summary

| Term | Detail |
|---|---|
| Principal | $52 million |
| Interest Rate | 13.5% per annum (cash pay) |
| Term | 36 months (matures March 2029) |
| Amortization | Interest-only for 18 months; then 2% quarterly principal |
| Origination Fee | 2.0% ($1.04M, deducted at close) |
| Prepayment Penalty | 3% in Year 1, 2% in Year 2, 1% in Year 3 |
| Security | First lien on all ARR contracts, IP, and bank accounts |
| Warrants | 3.5% warrant coverage (exercisable at $0.15/share) |
| **Financial Covenants** | |
| — Minimum ARR | $38M (must not fall below; tested quarterly) |
| — ARR Growth | Must maintain ≥10% YoY growth |
| — Gross Margin | Must remain ≥68% |
| — Minimum Cash | $3M at all times |
| — Debt / ARR | Must not exceed 1.35x |
| **Reporting Requirements** | Monthly financials, quarterly board observer seat for lender |

#### Financial Impact — Option A

**Annual debt service:**
- Year 1–1.5 (interest only): $52M × 13.5% = $7.02M/year ($585K/month)
- Year 1.5–3 (interest + 2% quarterly principal): ~$7.5M/year
- Total cash paid over 36 months: ~$22.8M in interest + fees

**Pro forma financials post-close (Option A):**

| Metric | Current | Post-Close (Month 1) | Month 12 | Month 24 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Cash | $9M | $12M ($9M + $4M net proceeds - $1M fees) | $4.8M (burn continues) | Depends on ARR growth |
| ARR | $41M | $41M | $48M (18% growth) | $57M |
| Monthly Burn (incl. debt service) | $1.1M | $1.23M | $1.23M | $1.0M (improving) |
| Runway | 8 months | ~10 months | — | — |
| Debt Outstanding | $48M | $52M | $52M | $49.9M |
| Debt / ARR | 1.17x | 1.27x | 1.08x | 0.88x |
| Equity Dilution from Warrants | — | 3.5% | 3.5% | 3.5% |

**Equity impact:**
- Existing shareholders retain 96.5% of equity (after warrant issuance)
- Valuation is not reset — last round valuation of $162M remains on record
- Employee options remain at current strike prices (some in-the-money if ARR growth re-rates the company)

**Covenant stress scenarios:**

| Scenario | ARR @ Month 12 | Covenant Breach? | Consequence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bull (25% growth) | $51.3M | No breach | Comfortable |
| Base (18% growth) | $48.4M | No breach | Tight on cash |
| Bear (10% growth) | $45.1M | ARR growth covenant breach | Acceleration clause triggered |
| Stress (5% growth) | $43.1M | Growth + Debt/ARR breach | Technical default |

#### Key Assumptions — Option A
- Meridian maintains ≥10% ARR growth through 2029 (minimum covenant threshold)
- Gross margin does not deteriorate below 68% (currently 72% — 400bps buffer)
- No major customer churn event that would breach the minimum ARR covenant of $38M
- Blue Arch does not exercise covenant acceleration for technical violations without negotiation
- New debt successfully retired by September 2026 before existing debt matures

#### Risks — Option A
1. **Covenant Breach Risk**: Any growth deceleration below 10% triggers acceleration — turns a 36-month instrument into an immediate repayment demand
2. **Cash Thin Risk**: Even with $12M post-close, burn of $1.23M/month gives only ~10 months of runway before ARR growth must fund operations — limited margin for error
3. **Debt Overhang Risk**: $52M debt on $41M ARR (1.27x Debt/ARR) is a significant overhang that may deter future equity investors or acquirers
4. **Interest Burden Risk**: $7M+ annual interest consumes 17% of ARR — a structural drag on the path to profitability
5. **Lien Risk**: First lien on ARR contracts gives Blue Arch significant leverage in any restructuring — equity holders are effectively subordinated
6. **Valuation Illusion Risk**: Preserving the $162M headline valuation is cosmetically appealing but economically meaningless if the company is distressed
7. **Refinancing Risk**: In 36 months, Meridian must refinance again — at what rate and under what conditions is unknown

### Option B: Discounted Equity Round — Series C at $95M Pre-Money

**Lead Investor:** Harmon Crossover Fund (crossover fund, $8.1B AUM)  
**Structure:** Preferred equity (Series C Preferred, 1x non-participating liquidation preference)  
**Amount:** $38 million  
**Pre-money valuation:** $95 million  
**Post-money valuation:** $133 million

#### Term Sheet Summary

| Term | Detail |
|---|---|
| Round Size | $38 million |
| Pre-Money Valuation | $95 million |
| Post-Money Valuation | $133 million |
| Price Per Share | $2.18 (vs. Series B price of $3.72) |
| Discount to Last Round | 41.4% markdown |
| Liquidation Preference | 1x non-participating |
| Anti-Dilution | Broad-based weighted average |
| Pro-Rata Rights | Yes (for Harmon in future rounds) |
| Board Seat | One new board seat for Harmon |
| Option Pool Top-Up | 8% refresh required pre-close (further dilutes existing holders) |
| Use of Proceeds | $48M debt retirement + $0M net (requires $5M bridge from existing investor + use of cash reserves — see below) |

**Note on proceeds gap:** $38M raises less than the $48M needed to fully retire debt. The deal structure includes a $5M bridge note from existing Series A investor (Foundry Group) at 8% converting into Series C equity at the Series C price at close. The remaining $5M gap is covered by Meridian's existing cash reserves, leaving the company with approximately $0M post-close (further reduced by operating burn during the 90–120 day closing period).

#### Dilution Analysis

**Pre- vs. Post-Transaction Cap Table (Option B):**

| Shareholder | Pre-Transaction % | Post-Transaction % | Change | Implied Value @ $133M Post-Money |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Founders (Priya + James) | 18.4% | 11.6% | -6.8% | $15.4M |
| Series A (Foundry Group) | 21.2% | 15.1% | -6.1% | $20.1M |
| Series B (Tiger Global) | 44.6% | 31.8% | -12.8% | $42.3M |
| Employee Option Pool (existing) | 11.8% | 7.4% | -4.4% | $9.8M |
| New Option Pool (8% refresh) | — | 8.0% | +8.0% | $10.6M |
| Harmon Crossover (Series C) | — | 22.6% | +22.6% | $30.1M |
| Foundry Bridge (converting) | — | 3.5% | +3.5% | $4.7M |
| Existing Warrants | 4.0% | 0.0% | -4.0% | Cancelled (negotiated) |
| **Total** | **100%** | **100%** | | **$133M** |

**Founder dilution impact:**
- Priya and James go from 18.4% → 11.6% combined
- At $133M post-money, their implied stake is worth $15.4M vs. $29.8M at the 2022 valuation — a paper loss of $14.4M
- However, at the prior $162M valuation, their stake was already largely theoretical given the debt overhang

**Employee option impact:**
- At $2.18/share (Series C price), options struck above $2.18 are underwater
- Estimated 74% of outstanding employee options are underwater post-round
- Retention risk: 31 employees with significant unvested options may consider leaving

#### Financial Impact — Option B

**Pro forma financials post-close (Option B):**

| Metric | Current | Post-Close (Month 1) | Month 12 | Month 24 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Cash | $9M | $0M (all proceeds retire debt; bridge provides working capital) | $7.2M | $16.8M |
| ARR | $41M | $41M | $48M | $57M |
| Monthly Burn | $1.1M | $1.1M | $0.95M | $0.7M |
| Runway | 8 months | ~24 months | — | — |
| Debt Outstanding | $48M | $0 | $0 | $0 |
| Debt / ARR | 1.17x | 0x | 0x | 0x |
| Interest Expense | $6.5M/yr | $0 | $0 | $0 |

**Note:** The bridge note from Foundry ($5M at 8%) converts to Series C equity at close — no ongoing cash interest obligation.

**Path to profitability (Option B):**
- Elimination of $7M annual interest expense immediately reduces burn by $583K/month
- Cash flow breakeven projected at Month 20 post-close (vs. Month 34 under Option A)
- Free cash flow positive by Month 26 (vs. never achieved under Option A base scenario)

#### Key Assumptions — Option B
- Harmon Crossover completes due diligence and confirms term sheet within 45 days
- Foundry Group agrees to participate with the $5M bridge note (pro-rata right exercised)
- Series B investors (Tiger Global) do not exercise blocking rights or demand co-sale protections that delay close
- Employee retention is managed through an option repricing program (estimated cost: $1.2M in additional equity)
- $95M pre-money valuation is defensible to customers, prospects, and press — down round narrative is managed

#### Risks — Option B
1. **Down Round Signal Risk**: A 41% markdown is publicly visible (in funding databases) and may raise questions from customers evaluating Meridian's long-term viability
2. **Employee Retention Risk**: 74% of options underwater; key engineers and salespeople may leave for better-capitalized competitors
3. **Founder Dilution Risk**: Founders drop to 11.6% — motivation and control implications are significant
4. **Investor Relations Risk**: Tiger Global (Series B lead) has been marked down severely; may push for a sale process rather than a down round
5. **Option Pool Refresh Dilution**: The 8% pool refresh required by Harmon further dilutes all existing holders beyond the headline round dilution
6. **Valuation Reset Permanence**: Once a down round is recorded, future up-round valuations must clear a higher bar to restore credibility — a $133M post-money means the next round must achieve $200M+ to look like recovery
7. **Closing Risk**: Complex cap table dynamics (warrants, bridge, pro-rata rights) increase closing risk — deals with this many moving parts fail to close ~25% of the time

3. **Marcus Fenn (Partner, Foundry Group — Series A lead)** — Favors Option B

4. **Rachel Kim (MD, Tiger Global — Series B lead)** — Ambivalent, leaning Option A

**The core trade-off in one sentence:** Option A bets that Meridian can grow fast enough to service expensive debt and satisfy tight covenants; Option B accepts immediate pain in exchange for a clean balance sheet and a longer runway to prove the business.

### Appendix B: Cost of Capital Analysis

**Option A — All-In Cost of Debt:**
- Cash interest: 13.5%
- Origination fee amortized over 3 years: +0.7%
- Warrant cost (3.5% dilution, valued at $133M implied): +~1.1%
- **Effective all-in cost: ~15.3%**

**Option B — Implied Cost of Equity:**
- Harmon pays $38M for 22.6% of a $133M post-money company
- Harmon's required return (crossover fund, 3–5 year horizon): 25–35% IRR
- At 30% IRR over 4 years, Harmon needs exit value of $38M × (1.3)⁴ = $109M on their stake
- Implies required company exit value: $109M / 22.6% = **$482M** (11.7x current ARR)
- This is achievable only if Meridian reaches $65–80M ARR and re-rates to 6–8x multiple

**Comparison:**
- Option A all-in cost: ~15.3% (known, fixed)
- Option B implied equity cost: 25–35% (uncertain, dependent on exit)
- On a pure cost-of-capital basis, debt is cheaper — but ignores covenant risk and distress probability

### Appendix C: Covenant Breach Probability Model

Assuming ARR growth follows a normal distribution with mean 18% and standard deviation 8%:

| Covenant | Trigger | Probability of Breach in 12 Months |
|---|---|---|
| ARR growth ≥10% | Growth falls below 10% | ~16% (1 standard deviation below mean) |
| Minimum ARR $38M | ARR falls below $38M | <2% (very unlikely at current base) |
| Gross Margin ≥68% | Margin falls below 68% | ~12% (competitive pricing pressure) |
| Minimum cash $3M | Cash falls below $3M | ~35% (given burn rate and thin post-close cash) |

*The minimum cash covenant has the highest breach probability and is the most operationally binding constraint under Option A.*
